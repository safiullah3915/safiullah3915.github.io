Research

My research centers on understanding why machine learning models fail to generalize across institutions, markets, and distributions, and on building diagnostic tools that catch this failure before deployment.

Research Interests

  • Robustness and generalization of ML systems under distribution shift
  • Transfer learning and domain adaptation across sequential and relational data
  • Attention-based and graph neural network architectures
  • Trustworthy AI for high-stakes and critical-infrastructure systems
  • Cross-domain knowledge transfer in high-stakes real-world applications, including finance, security, and healthcare
  • Adversarial domain adaptation and domain-shift quantification (MMD)

Research Vision

The cross-market transfer learning work below is one instance of a broader question I am interested in: when and why does a model’s measured performance fail to transfer once the underlying data distribution shifts. The same diagnostic toolkit used there, Maximum Mean Discrepancy-based distributional distance measures paired with Friedman testing, Bonferroni-corrected Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, and Cohen’s d effect sizes, applies just as naturally to other high-stakes settings, including treatment-effect estimation and diagnostic prediction in healthcare, training-data composition effects in foundation model evaluation, and the relationship between model multiplicity and distribution shift in interpretable machine learning. I am interested in extending this statistical-rigor-first approach to whichever domain a given research group is already working in, rather than treating finance as the only setting where these questions matter.

Current Research

Cross-Market Equity Transfer Learning

Dec 2025 – Present
Quaid-i-Azam University, Islamabad, Pakistan
  • Designed a six-stage end-to-end ML pipeline for cross-market transfer learning between India (NSE/BSE, 16 companies) and Pakistan (PSX, 15 companies) across four sectors, covering 125,668 daily observations over a 23-year window (2000–2023).
  • Proposed a CNN-BiLSTM-Attention architecture with causally-padded convolutions, correcting temporal-leakage issues present in standard zero-padding implementations used by prior financial forecasting systems.
  • Identified a first-documented directional transfer asymmetry (+2.06 pp, Pakistan→India) through a five-scenario evaluation framework: full-market pooling, sector-conditioned 4×4 Transferability Matrix, and progressive fine-tuning experiments.
  • Applied Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) domain-shift analysis across all 16 sector combinations; confirmed near-random directional accuracy consistent with semi-strong Efficient Market Hypothesis in South Asian emerging markets.
  • Validated findings with Friedman test, Bonferroni-corrected Wilcoxon signed-rank tests (n = 31), and Cohen's d effect sizes, ensuring statistical rigor suitable for peer-reviewed submission.

Publications & Manuscripts

S. Ullah. “Cross-Market Transfer Learning for Equity Price Prediction: An Empirical Study of India–Pakistan Transfer Dynamics.”
Manuscript under preparation · 2025
  • First systematic empirical study of cross-market transfer learning between Indian and Pakistani equity markets.
  • Compares six model families (Naïve Persistence, ARIMA, XGBoost, Vanilla LSTM, Vanilla GRU, and the proposed CNN-BiLSTM-Attention) across 32 companies, four sectors, and five transfer scenarios.
